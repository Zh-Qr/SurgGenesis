SurgGenesis:A Generative Surgical World Model for Future-Aware Surgical Understanding

Zh-Qr and SurgGenesis Contributors

GitHub Results

Abstract

A medical world model should learn the underlying dynamics and state transitions of a clinical environment from observation. Building such a model for surgery is challenging due to complex tool–tissue interactions, non-rigid anatomical dynamics, and limited annotated data. We propose SurgGenesis, a generative surgical world model that learns future surgical state transitions in a latent space from visual observations and textual procedural descriptions. The framework models long-range temporal evolution in a unified latent space and progressively incorporates procedural semantics and spatial motion cues to improve temporal coherence and predictive consistency. The learned predictive representation captures both visual appearance and underlying surgical dynamics, providing a shared foundation for multiple future-oriented tasks. Specifically, latent-space decoding enables future semantic segmentation and triplet recognition, while observation-space processing provides interpretable trajectory forecasting and geometry reconstruction from generated future observations. This design bridges generative forecasting and structured surgical understanding, enabling comprehensive understanding of evolving surgical environments. Beyond generation quality, we introduce a decoupled validation protocol---pairing a GT-latent prediction gap with downstream evaluation---to test whether the predicted futures are actually usable. Experiments show progressive gains in video fidelity and temporal consistency, and demonstrate that the generated data acts as a surgical data engine, improving long-tail triplet recognition while supporting segmentation, 3D reconstruction, and a trajectory readout.
